How asphalt can benefit your business

Asphalt is one of the most versatile materials for paving roads, driveways, and parking lots. But did you know that it also has a lot of benefits for businesses?

Here are some reasons why your business should consider using asphalt.

Read Post

Asphalt is durable.

Asphalt is a challenging and durable material, which is why it’s often used for high-traffic areas such as roads and parking lots. It can withstand a lot of wear and tear, which means it will last a long time with proper maintenance.

However, even the most challenging asphalt surfaces can show signs of wear and tear over time. If you notice any cracks, potholes, or other damage to your asphalt, it must be repaired as soon as possible. Neglecting repairs can lead to further injury and may require a complete replacement.

Asphalt is low-maintenance.

Asphalt doesn’t require a lot of upkeep, which is another cost-saving benefit for businesses. Once it’s installed, you won’t have to do much to maintain it other than occasional sealing and repairs. This contrasts with other materials, such as concrete, which require frequent cleaning and repairs.

Since asphalt is a flexible material, it’s less likely to crack under extreme temperature changes or heavy traffic. This means you can enjoy a low-maintenance parking lot or driveway for many years.

Asphalt is an excellent option to consider if you’re looking for a durable, cost-effective, and low-maintenance paving solution for your business.

Asphalt is slip-resistant.

Another advantage of asphalt is that it’s slip-resistant, even when wet. This means it can provide a safe surface for driving, walking, and biking in all weather conditions.

Asphalt is also very durable, so that it can withstand a lot of wear and tear. It’s not uncommon for asphalt driveways and roads to last for decades with proper maintenance. And if any repairs are needed, they are usually relatively straightforward to make.

These factors make asphalt an excellent choice for residential and commercial applications. Ask your contractor about using asphalt if you’re considering paving your driveway or parking lot. It just might be the perfect solution for you.

You can recycle asphalt.

Did you know that asphalt is one of the most recycled materials? Over 80% of asphalt pavement is recycled each year. This means that when your business needs to replace its asphalt, it can reuse the old material instead of going into a landfill.

There are several reasons to consider using recycled asphalt for your business. For one, it is more economical than using new materials. It is also better for the environment since it reduces the need for mining and quarrying operations.

Recycled asphalt can be used in several different applications, including:


  1. Asphalt Paving: Recycled asphalt can be used as a paving material for driveways, parking lots, and roadways. It is durable and long lasting, making it ideal for high-traffic areas.

  2. Asphalt Roofing: Asphalt roofing shingles are made from recycled asphalt, providing a sustainable option for your business.

  3. Asphalt Siding: Asphalt siding is a durable and long lasting alternative to traditional siding materials. It is also resistant to rot, insects, and UV rays.

  4. Asphalt Landscaping: Asphalt can be used in several landscaping applications, such as pathways, retaining walls, and edging.

  5. Asphalt Pavement Recycling: When asphalt pavement is removed, it can be recycled and used as a base for new pavement. This helps to reduce the need for quarrying operations and the associated environmental impacts.


As you can see, there are several reasons to consider using recycled asphalt for your business needs. It is an economical and sustainable option used in various applications.

Asphalt is a sustainable choice.

Asphalt is a sustainable material because it can be recycled and doesn’t require a lot of resources to produce. It’s also durable, so it will last a long time, reducing the environmental impact of construction projects.

When it comes to sustainability, asphalt is an intelligent choice. It can be recycled and reused, so it doesn’t require new resources to produce. Asphalt is also durable, so it will last a long time, reducing the environmental impact of construction projects.
